About

Hi, I am a Master's student at the University of Pennsylvania, working in natural language processing. My research interests range from building interactive agents in different scenarios to understanding and utilizing reasoning capabilities of large language models to boarder areas. Most of my skills are built around natural language processing and reinforcement learning.

During my research journey, I am super lucky to be guided by my amazing mentors, Prof. Alane Suhr and Prof. Nicholas Tomlin at Berkeley NLP Group, Prof. Mark Yatskar at Penn NLP Group, and Prof. Graham Neubig and Dr. Xiang Yue at CMU LTI. With their guidance and support, I have been mainly working on developing multimodal agents and understanding reasoning behaviors of current LLMs.

My original name could be hard to pronounce sometimes. Happy to call me Maggie or Ziyu (IPA: /ts…ØÀá. yÀã/) if you like, I usually react to both of them.
